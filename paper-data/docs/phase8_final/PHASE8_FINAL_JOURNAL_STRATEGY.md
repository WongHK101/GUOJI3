# Phase 8 Final Journal Strategy

## Primary target: Knowledge-Based Systems

The strongest fit is knowledge-reliability auditing rather than a new
state-of-the-art causal-discovery method. The paper asks when a neural
Granger-causality pipeline's extracted graph remains a defensible knowledge
object. Its reusable contribution is the route/coordinate/horizon audit,
supported by replicated failure evidence and a transparent negative repair
boundary.

The submission should not be marketed as an ISTF, Mamba, filtering, or benchmark
paper. The title, abstract, figures, cover letter, and highlights should use the
same audit framing.

## Main reviewer risk

Reviewers may view the work as predominantly negative. The response is not to
hide the no-go result. The manuscript should lead with the constructive audit
framework, the replicated prediction--knowledge decoupling, and the reusable
declaration/checklist. The bounded repair study then demonstrates why semantic
coverage and method competitiveness must be evaluated separately.

## Fallback venues

- Neural Networks, if positioned around neural Granger score semantics and
  original-input attribution.
- Engineering Applications of Artificial Intelligence, if positioned as an AI
  reliability and model-audit study.

No venue strategy should restore unsupported benchmark or method-superiority
claims.
