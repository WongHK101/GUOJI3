# Route-B Draft v2.3 Remaining Strategic Risks

These risks are recorded for journal strategy. V2.3 does not attempt to close
them through new experiments.

## 1. KBS contribution-strength risk

The manuscript's constructive contribution is an audit framework and a
score-semantics argument rather than a competitive new causal-discovery method.
KBS reviewers may value knowledge-reliability auditing, but some may still
expect a stronger method-positive contribution or broader empirical validation.

## 2. Controlled-diagnostic replication risk

Several central controlled diagnostics remain single-run evidence: the
auxiliary-capacity sweep, mask/shuffle intervention, and coefficient-recovery
diagnostic. Their claims are explicitly bounded to those constructions, but
the limited replication can still reduce reviewer confidence in generality.

## 3. Positive-repair risk

The pre-specified coordinate-preserving CP-depthwise repair passed semantic
gates but failed both the performance gate and FixedFIR3 novelty gate. The
paper therefore lacks a competitive positive repair method and cannot promise
that the audit directly yields an empirically superior architecture.

## 4. Journal-fit fallback

If KBS judges the audit contribution insufficiently method-positive, the most
plausible fallback venues are Neural Networks for a neural-score semantics and
attribution framing, or Engineering Applications of Artificial Intelligence
for an AI reliability and auditing framing. This is a strategy contingency,
not a claim that either venue will accept the current draft.

## Locked response

These risks must be handled by framing, claim calibration, and venue choice at
the next adversarial review. They must not be hidden, converted into positive
claims, or addressed by unplanned experiments within the v2.3 science lock.
